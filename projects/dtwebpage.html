dtweb.page is my website that holds a record of my projects/work/contact/everything. It will basically always be an Ongoing project forever. And ever.

This is the third iteration of the site.


Version One (2023-10-30)

The first iteration was extremely barebones, consisting of literally one page. I had a similar pixel aesthetic going, but with different colours. I think I made it in one day...

Archived version Okay, for whatever reason the source HTML totally messed up, so my CSS doesn't line up with the ‹div› classes. Consider it lost media.


Version Two (2024-05-03)

This iteration was very short-lived, and also really boring. I just slapped Inter, Solarized Light, and a few flexbox columns on it, and called it a day.

Archived version


Version Three (2024-05-30)

The current version. Inspired by terminal interfaces, and old websites designed for smaller screens.

Vertical space is prioritized here. The site minimizes how much horizontal space is used for better line readability.

The small bordering lines are something I've tried for all three iterations, but I think this is the only one where it works. Tight 'tolerances' with the defining lines make it looked 'machined' or 'manufactured' in a cyber-punkish manner.

It's almost meant to be an 'interface' or 'screen' to me, my site, my work.

Pretention aside, it looks cool.
